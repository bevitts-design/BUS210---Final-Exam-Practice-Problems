TVM Problems

TVM #1: Retirement Savings

The Question: You are 35 years old and starting to plan for retirement. You have $10,000 to roll over from an old job and you plan to add $500 every month. If you earn an 8.00% return, how much will you have in 30 years?

Problem Data:
  • Current Starting Amount (PV): $10,000
  • Monthly Savings (PMT): $500
  • Annual Interest Rate: 8.00%
  • Time: 30 years
  • Compounding: Monthly (12 times a year)

TVM #2: Car Loan Monthly Payment

The Question: You bought a new car for $25,000. You are paying it off over 5 years with an 8.00% APR. What is your monthly payment, and how much total interest will you pay over the life of the loan?

Problem Data:
  • Loan Amount (PV): $25,000
  • Annual Rate (APR): 8.00%
  • Term: 5 Years (60 months)
  • Compounding: Monthly

Bond Problems

Bond #1: Zero-Coupon Bond

The Question: Calculate the dollar bond value of a zero-coupon bond that matures in 10 years if the market interest rate is 0.00%.

  • Years to Maturity: 10
  • Market Rate: 0.00%
  • Compounding: Semiannual
  • Par Value: $1,000

Bond #2: Coupon Bond and Total Return

The Question: A 15-year bond with semi-annual coupon payments and a 6.00% coupon rate is currently valued at $1,000 (100.00% price).

  • What is the initial yield on the bond?
  • After receiving the first coupon payment, you sell the bond at a yield of 5.00%. What is the price (PV) of the bond at the time of the sale?
  • Calculate the capital gain, interest earned, total dollar return, and total percentage return.
Problem Data:
  • Coupon Rate: 6.00%
  • Years to Maturity: 15
  • Compounding: Semi-annual (2x per year)
  • Par Value: $1,000
  • Sale Yield: 0.00%

Bond #3: Municipal Bond Yields & Taxable Equivalent

The Question: A 4.50% coupon municipal bond has 15 years left to maturity and has a price quote of 98.45%. The bond can be called in five years. The call premium is one year of coupon payments. (Assume semiannual payments and a $1,000 par value.)

Your Tasks:
  • Compute the bond’s current yield.
  • Compute the yield to maturity (YTM).
  • Compute the taxable equivalent yield (for an investor in the 36% marginal tax bracket).
  • Compute the yield to call (YTC).
Problem Data:
  • Coupon Rate: 4.50%
  • Years to Maturity: 15
  • Current Price: 98.45% ($984.50)
  • Time to Call: 5 years
  • Call Premium: $45.00 (1 year of coupons)
  • Tax Bracket: 36%
  • Compounding: Semiannual
